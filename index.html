About Me

I am currently a Master's candidate at the School of Artificial Intelligence, Nanjing University (NJU), and a member of the NLP research group, led by Prof. Jiajun Chen. My advisor is Prof. Xinyu Dai.

My academic journey is driven by a passion for advancing the frontiers of artificial intelligence, particularly in developing more intelligent and reasoning-capable systems that can understand and interact with the world in meaningful ways.

Research Interests

Large Language Models

Exploring the capabilities and limitations of large-scale language models, focusing on their reasoning abilities and practical applications.

Reinforcement Learning

Investigating reinforcement learning techniques to enhance model reasoning and decision-making capabilities in complex environments.

In-Context Learning

Studying how models can learn and adapt from contextual information without explicit parameter updates.

Publications

Text and Table Numerical Question-answering Model Based on Multi-granularity Cell Contrast

Authors: Jiang-Zhou Ju, Yun-Lin Mao, Zhen Wu, Yu-Fei Chen, Xin-Yu Dai, Jia-Jun Chen

This paper presents a novel approach to numerical question-answering tasks involving both text and tables, introducing a multi-granularity cell contrast method to improve the model's ability to distinguish between relevant and irrelevant information in tabular data.
